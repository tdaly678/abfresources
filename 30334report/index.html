Goals Achieved · Q9 Synthesis

AI-synthesized from 6 of 14 substantive responses
AI-generated · Goal-achievement reflection

Six respondents wrote substantive answers about goals from the series. Four (Casey, Eileen, Heather, Dan F) named concrete biblical-application goals they took from the teaching — Casey on stewardship of what Christ has provided his family, Eileen on identifying and applying "what it means to be rich towards God," Heather on being thoughtful "in God's economy," Dan F specifically calling the financial/retirement goals "fantastic."

Two respondents (May, Bethany) wrote that they're still working through the goals. May: "still working on them." Bethany surfaced a real tension: balancing current spiritual goals with new ones from the series that may not be the most urgent focus right now.

  • "Identify + apply what it means to be rich towards God." — Eileen
  • "Goals based in biblical application. Especially w/ focusing on what Christ has provided my family w/ & being stewards of it." — Casey
  • "Mostly reading passages, and was good. But also gave financial/retirement goals that were fantastic." — Dan F

Highlights · Q13 Synthesis

AI-synthesized from 13 of 14 highlight responses
AI-generated · Standout-moments synthesis

Two highlight clusters dominate. Practical content depth — estate planning, financial/legal representation, kids' education stewardship — was named by 5 of 13 respondents as the standout substance. Discussion and reflection time was named by 4 respondents as the standout format, with specific callouts to the table conversations and group reflective moments.

One unusual signal: Bates wrote "I looked forward to this coming each week" — a forward-looking response to a retrospective question. None of the other 13 respondents wrote about anticipation between sessions.

  • "Practical Hows of Finances + Scripture to Support them." — Jen
  • "discussion showed it was impactful throughout." — Heather
  • "Estate planning, final stages of life." — Nick
  • "I really liked group reflective times." — Bethany
